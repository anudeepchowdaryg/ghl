Visual Animation Sequence
Initial State (Hero Load):
Text: The primary headline "Find What Moves You" is displayed in solid, high-contrast typography.
Image: A large building image is anchored at the bottom of the viewport, partially obscured by the bottom edge. The background is a soft sky/cloudscape.
Initial Scroll (0 - 300px):
Fade-Out: The "Find What Moves You" text begins to fade away smoothly.
Parallax: The building starts to move upward at a faster rate than the scroll (parallax), giving a sense of depth and massive scale.
The Outline Reveal (300 - 800px):
New Typography: A secondary text layer, "FIND Real Estate," fades in.
Stroke Effect: Initially, this text is rendered only as a white outline (stroke) with a transparent fill.
Interaction: The building continues to rise, passing behind the outlined letters. At this stage, you can see the building through the transparent center of the "FIND" letters.
The Masking Transition (800 - 1500px):
The "Pop": This is the climax of the effect. As you scroll further, a seamless transition occurs.
Background Fade: The building image in the background (outside the text) begins to dissolve or fade in opacity.
Text Fill: Simultaneously, the "FIND Real Estate" letters transition from a stroke to a masked fill. The building image appears "trapped" inside the letters.
Final Result:
The background building completely disappears, leaving only the sky.
The text "FIND Real Estate" is now a window (mask) through which the building texture is visible. The texture perfectly aligns with where the building was just a moment before, creating a "locking" effect.
Technical Analysis & CSS Techniques
Based on the behavior, the site likely uses the following techniques:

Dual Layering: There are likely two identical building images. One is a standard background/img layer, and the other is a hidden layer used for the text fill.
background-clip: text or SVG Masking:
The "FIND Real Estate" text likely uses background-clip: text with a background image of the building.
Alternatively, an SVG <mask> or CSS mask-image is used. This allows the building to be visible only within the bounds of the text paths.
Scroll-Linked Animation (JS):
The movement is tied to window.scrollY. Libraries like GSAP (ScrollTrigger) or Framer Motion are commonly used for this level of precision.
Opacity Mapping: The stroke opacity and background building opacity are likely mapped to specific scroll ranges (e.g., opacity: 1 at 800px, opacity: 0 at 1200px).
Parallax Transform: Both the background building and the building image inside the text move using transform: translateY() but at slightly different multipliers to maintain the "window" illusion.
